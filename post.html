Man must explore, and this is exploration at its greatest

Problems look mighty small when your success is so high

Posted by Micah Hennegan on August 24, 2018

I want to make an impact in the world. Living on a such a small island I come to find that I was a big fish living in a small pond. Coming to America has changed my view-point on everything. 

I am not saying that I am going to disregard my island traditions and upbringings. I want to actually be an ambassador for my island and actually put Guam on the map where everyone can see it. 

My plan is to get my voice as far and wide as I can. In college I want to learn how to be a business man while being a  Christian in this world. 

Currently, I am learning valuable classes such as Human Resources, Business 101, and Digital Literacy to expound my knowledge. 

I also am learning core classes such as English, Speech, Bible Literature, and many more.

Heading

I am a man with endless opportunities and paths that are in front of me to take. I am in search for wisdom and guidance so that I can know what I want to do with life. 

I believe that coming to a Christian University, I am able to learn many valuable things. I am at a stage in my life where I am starting to find identity in myself. 

I want to be ready for the world and its challenges it will bring to me. 

To go places and do things that have never been done before – that’s what living is all about.

As I stand out here in the wonders of the unknown at Bob Jones, I sort of realize there’s a fundamental truth to our nature, Man must explore, and this is exploration at its greatest.
